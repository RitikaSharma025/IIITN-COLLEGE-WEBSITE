Student Life

Don’t Be Just a Student, Be a Panther

Visit us to know more


Life at IIITN is More Than Just Going to Class

Walk to the Graham center and join a club, or go outside and take a seat with the yoga class on the lawn. Join a fraternity or sorority and go Greek, or if you have the voice, apply at our radio station: the Roar. Here at IIITN, we encourage you to get out there and become a member of the IIITN family.


Student Involment

We're not just talkers, we're doers. Getting involved is about more than getting to know your 4,000 fellow students. As Panthers, we work together to make a difference and we're always improving. Find your people and start growing in any of our 10+ clubs and organizations.

Campus

Your next adventure is on campus. Kayak offshore of Biscayne Bay Campus (BBC) among the mangroves. Spend a day roaming through over 300 acres of the lush Modesto A. Maidique Campus (MMC). There's so much to explore on campus. You need to see it for yourself.

Arts & Culture

The Patricia & Phillip Frost Art Museum is open every day except Monday, so check out one of their exhibits. Immerse yourself in our Theatre department’s original stage plays. And there's always a concert going on at our School of Music.

Recreation

Whether you’re looking for a great workout, an outdoor experience or the rush of competing, FIU’s recreation options inspire a fit and healthy lifestyle. From ropes courses to group classes to weights, there's something for everyone.



Events

Year-round Fun in the Tropical Sun


Here in South Florida the seasons don't change too much. But you'll know it's fall when football, volleyball and soccer season arrive. It's also time for Homecoming, when you join your FIU family at tailgates and in the stands to cheer our athletes, unleash your panther spirit at Panthermonium Concert and so much more.


In spring and summer, you can dance all night at Roarthon or party at Summerfest. Year-round, you'll see musicians, celebrities and inspiring individuals who come to speak or perform right here on campus.
